[**Netflix**](https://screenrant.com/tag/netflix/) keeps bringing in major stars for its movies and TV shows, with a returning thriller series growing to feature more top-notch talent, cementing its name among the best in the streamer's history when it comes to ensembles. For a show to succeed, it needs to be strong in different areas. Story, cinematography, VFX, and more are some of those, but casting is often the most vital.

After all, if the audience does not connect with a show's characters, they will not press play beyond the first episode. That is something [Netflix's Original series](https://screenrant.com/netflix-shows-everyone-should-watch/) understand quite well. *Stranger Things*, *The Crown*, *Black Mirror*, and more showcase the streaming service's ability to either attract major stars or birth new names that have gone on to do great things in the industry.

[Netflix's thriller series](https://screenrant.com/netflix-best-thriller-tv-shows-ranked/) have done a good job with their casting department, nabbing stars like Keira Knightley, Jason Bateman, Emma Stone, Jude Law, Jon Bernthal, Rachel Weisz, and many more. Among those is [***Beef***](https://screenrant.com/db/tv-show/beef/), which had an award-winning behemoth of a first season, soon set to return for season 2 in an anthology format that allows its cast to keep changing and rotating major stars.

## This Netflix Thriller Series Started With A Phenomenal Cast

![Danny (Steven Yeun) and Amy (Ali Wong) in Beef leaning out of their cars.](https://static0.srcdn.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/new-project-46.jpg?q=70&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=1)

Danny (Steven Yeun) and Amy (Ali Wong) in Beef leaning out of their cars.

*Beef* season 1, which debuted on Netflix on April 6, 2023, told the story of Danny Cho and Amy Lau. Initially strangers, their destinies became intertwined when a road rage incident started a legendary feud that took control of their lives. The rivalry was perfect on paper, with *Beef* 's sharp writing leading to a series of wild events that just kept escalating.

However, the Netflix thriller series was only a major hit because of its cast. *Beef* season 1's ensembles featured names like Academy Award nominee Steven Yeun, Emmy Award winner Ali Wong, Emmy Award nominee Young Mazino, and more, like Maria Bello (ER), Ashley Park (Emily in Paris), and Justin H. Min (The Umbrella Academy).

*Beef* season 1 had absolute tour de force performances from Yeun and Wong in the lead roles, making Danny and Amy as charming or as infuriating as needed. They succeeded in crafting complex characters whom the audience alternated between hating and loving at the flick of a wrist. The larger ensemble served their feud with entertaining side characters, hated villains, and more.

## Beef Season 2's New Cast Shows The Netflix Series' Impressive Pull

After three years, [*Beef* season 2](https://screenrant.com/beef-season-2-netflix-show-cast-details-updates/) will be released on Netflix on April 16. With the show taking on an anthology format, viewers can expect entirely new casts or mostly new players for every season of the Netflix thriller series. However, the quality of the cast is never dropping, which the arrival of Oscar Isaac, Carey Mulligan, Charles Melton, and Cailee Spaeny in lead roles guarantees.

Melton and Spaeny's Austin Davis and Ashley Miller, a young couple working at a country club, watch an intense fight unfold between their married bosses, Isaac's Josh Martín and Mulligan's Lindsay Crane-Martín. What starts then is a blackmail battle that promises to go to wild heights, just like the Netflix series' first season.

*Beef* season 2 expands the feud to four characters instead of just two, exploring the potential between two couples of very different backgrounds. By casting a duo of established Hollywood veterans, Isaac and Mulligan, opposite a couple of rising stars, Melton (May December) and Spaeny (Priscilla), it keeps things fresh, switching up ***Beef*** 's formula to keep the show's momentum with another star-studded cast.
