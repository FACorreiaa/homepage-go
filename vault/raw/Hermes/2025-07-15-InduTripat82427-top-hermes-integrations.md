# Top Hermes Integrations to Give Your Agent Superpowers

An infographic titled "Top Hermes Integrations" showcases 12 third-party tools and platforms that can be integrated with Hermes (an AI agent platform) to enhance its functionality.

## The 12 Integrations

1. **Obsidian** — Perfect for a second-brain setup; turns Hermes into a useful assistant across your personal knowledge base.
2. **Reddit** — Best place to see what real users actually think about a product, niche, or problem.
3. **InsForge** — The agentic backend; gives Hermes auth, database, storage, and functions through one semantic layer.
4. **GitHub** — Gives Hermes access to code, issues, and PRs; essential if you write software.
5. **Firecrawl** — Agent-first web search; pulls clean page data fast so Hermes uses fewer tokens and returns better answers.
6. **YouTube Transcripts** — Turns videos, podcasts, and interviews into searchable text for faster research.
7. **Google Workspace** — Connects Gmail, Calendar, Drive, Docs, and Sheets, so Hermes can actually work across your day-to-day systems.
8. **Discord** — Great for channel-based workflows; e.g., Hermes can scan support emails and drop organized tickets into a support channel.
9. **Stripe** — Lets Hermes inspect payments, customers, refunds, and failed charges; ask why someone churned and get a real answer.
10. **Bland or Twilio** — Gives Hermes a voice so it can place real phone calls, like booking reservations.
11. **Graphiti (by Zep)** — Real-time knowledge graphs for agents; lets Hermes build and query structured memory from conversations and documents.
12. **FireFlies** — Searchable meeting transcripts; great for recalling what a client said last month.

## Relevance ✅

Nearly every listed integration is already connected to your Hermes setup:
- **ObsidianBrain** (your Obsidian vault) — #1, exactly matches your second-brain project
- **GitHub** — active dev workflow
- **YouTube Transcripts** — backed by your `youtube-link-ingestor` cron
- **Discord/Telegram** — both connected and actively used
- **Google Workspace** — documented as available
- **Firecrawl / FireFlies** — possible future additions worth evaluating
