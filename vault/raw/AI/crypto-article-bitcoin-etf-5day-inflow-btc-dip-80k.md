# Bitcoin ETFs snap 5-day inflow streak as BTC dips under $80K

[Latest News](https://cointelegraph.com/category/latest-news)Published May 8, 2026

Bitcoin ETFs snap a five-day $1.7 billion inflow streak with $277.5 million of outflows as Bitcoin falls below $80,000 amid sharp intraday volatility.

 Copy link 


US-listed spot Bitcoin (BTC) exchange-traded funds (ETFs) snapped a five-day inflow streak totaling nearly $1.7 billion as Bitcoin dipped below $80,000.

Bitcoin funds logged $277.5 million in outflows on Thursday, marking the first daily outflows in May, [according](https://sosovalue.com/assets/etf/us-btc-spot) to SoSoValue data.


_Daily spot Bitcoin ETF flows since Friday. Source: SoSoValue_

The Fidelity Wise Origin Bitcoin Fund (FBTC) led the outflows at $129 million, while BlackRock’s iShares Bitcoin Trust ETF (IBIT) followed with $98 million in outflows, [according](https://farside.co.uk/btc/) to Farside.

The sharp reversal in Bitcoin ETF flows came amid heightened Bitcoin volatility. Bitcoin [rose](https://www.coingecko.com/en/coins/bitcoin) above $82,000 on Wednesday before falling below the key $80,000 level the next day.

## Morgan Stanley’s Bitcoin ETF remains resilient amid broader outflows

The Morgan Stanley Bitcoin Trust ETF (MSBT), the first spot Bitcoin ETF launched by a US bank, recorded modest inflows of $7.3 million on Thursday. The fund has not seen a single day of outflows [since debut on April 8](https://cointelegraph.com/news/morgan-stanley-bitcoin-etf-trails-blackrock-with-30m-in-first-day-inflows), 2026, according to Farside.

MSBT has so far accumulated 2,920 BTC, worth around $232.6 million, growing assets held for its customers by 557% since launch.


_Daily spot Bitcoin ETF flows by issuer (in millions of US dollars) since Friday. Source: SoSoValue_

The only other Bitcoin fund to record inflows on the day was the Grayscale Bitcoin Mini Trust ETF (BTC), a low-cost spot Bitcoin ETF offered by Grayscale alongside its Grayscale Bitcoin Trust (GBTC).

## Canton Network ETF ends slightly lower on Nasdaq debut as token slips

The Bitcoin ETF outflows came alongside the Nasdaq [debut](https://x.com/21shares_us/status/2052381153684754447) of the 21Shares Canton Network ETF (TCAN), the first US-listed ETF designed to offer direct exposure to Canton Coin, the native utility token of the Canton Network.

TCAN began trading on Nasdaq on Thursday and closed its first session at $24.66, slightly down from an initial price of $24.76, [according](https://www.nasdaq.com/market-activity/etf/tcan/advanced-charting) to Nasdaq data. Canton Coin slipped 1.7% on the day, trading at $0.145 at the time of writing, [according](https://www.coingecko.com/en/coins/canton) to CoinGecko.


_The Crypto Fear & Greed Index. Source:_[_Alternative.me_](https://alternative.me/crypto/fear-and-greed-index/)

The negative trend in crypto markets pushed the Crypto Fear & Greed Index into “Fear” on Friday at 38 after briefly returning to “Neutral” the previous day. The index is still significantly above April levels, when it averaged 17, as Bitcoin has risen about 11% over the past 30 days.
