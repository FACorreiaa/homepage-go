**Alpha Batcher** @alphabatcher 2026-05-04

People keep trying to build AI trading bots

the better product is an AI research memory

trading bots hallucinate, research memories compound

Hermes becomes useful when it has three things:

1\. source flow

\> X accounts

\> bookmarks

\> news

\> dashboards

\> market data

\> prediction markets

2\. personal context

\> positions

\> theses

\> risk appetite

\> watchlist

\> entry reasons

\> exit targets

\> past mistakes

3\. recurring synthesis

\> daily brief

\> weekly pattern review

\> stress test

\> alert layer

\> thesis update

that is why this setup is different from asking ChatGPT for market takes

ChatGPT gives you a generic answer

Hermes can answer inside your actual life

“this matters because you already hold X”

“this contradicts thesis 2”

“this is relevant to the exit level you set last week”

“you said you tend to baghold this kind of trade”

that is better memory plus better inputs

the analyst is not valuable because it predicts the future

it is valuable because it watches the right things every day and remembers what you told it yesterday

the product idea here is obvious:

not AI that trades for you

AI that stops you from missing the signal you already said mattered
