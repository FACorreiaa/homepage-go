How to start making money with your Hermes Agent

If your AI agent is not making you money, it is still just a chatbot.

Your AI Agent should pay for itself.

Not someday. Not after you build a giant autonomous company. Not after you spend 3 months watching YouTube tutorials.

This week.

The next wave of one-person businesses will not be built with employees. They will be built with personal agents.

Most people are going to approach this completely wrong. They will ask: "What can my agent automate?"

That is the wrong starting point. The better question is: "What useful outcome can my agent produce every week that someone would pay for?"

That is where the money starts.

Agents do not make money because they are autonomous. They make money because they repeatedly solve painful bottlenecks.

A business does not pay for "an AI agent." It pays for:
- more qualified leads
- better research
- faster follow-up
- cleaner reports
- fewer missed opportunities
- useful market alerts
- content ideas that are actually worth posting

So if you want to make your first $100 to $1,000 with Hermes, do not sell the agent. Sell the output.

## 5 Starter Offers You Can Build With Hermes

### 1. Weekly Niche Intelligence Brief

Pick a niche where people need to know what changed. AI tools. Crypto markets. Local real estate. Hiring trends. Funding rounds. YouTube niches. Competitor launches. Regulatory updates.

Your Hermes Agent can monitor sources, read articles, summarize changes, filter noise, and deliver a clean weekly brief.

You are not selling "research." You are selling: "Here are the 10 things you should know this week, why they matter, and what to do next."

Start with one niche. Find 5 people who care. Send them one free sample. Charge for the second one.

### 2. Sales Prep Service

Most sales teams do not need more software. They need better prep.

Your Hermes Agent can:
- find target accounts
- research the company
- identify buying signals
- summarize the opportunity
- draft a personalized outreach message
- organize everything in a spreadsheet or CRM

The human still approves the message. That is the point. Do not sell full autonomy. Sell supervised leverage.

A simple offer: "I will deliver 50 researched leads per week with custom outreach angles."

### 3. Competitor Intelligence

Every company says they care about competitors. Very few actually track them properly.

Your Hermes Agent can watch: pricing pages, product pages, job listings, social posts, ads, newsletters, changelogs, funding news, app updates.

Then it can send a weekly report: "What changed?" "Why does it matter?" "What should you do?"

### 4. Content Research System

Most founders know they should post. They just do not know what to say.

Your Hermes Agent can track trends, study outlier posts, collect examples, summarize news, and turn raw ideas into drafts.

Not generic AI slop. Useful angles. The better offer is: "I run your content research system every week so you never start from a blank page."

### 5. Market Alerts / Decision Support

Trading agents. Crypto agents. Market agents. Wallet monitoring. News alerts. Price movement. On-chain signals.

But do not start by giving an agent real money. Start with alerts.

Your Hermes Agent can monitor markets, APIs, wallets, news, filings, tickers, or specific events. Then it can send: what happened, why it matters, what conditions triggered the alert, what to check before acting.

First alerts. Then simulations. Then paper trading. Then strict limits. Execution comes later.

## The 7-Day Approach

Day 1: Pick one painful outcome.
Day 2: Build the Hermes workflow manually.
Day 3: Turn the workflow into a repeatable checklist.
Day 4: Create one sample deliverable.
Day 5: Send it to 10 people who actually have the problem.
Day 6: Charge for version two.
Day 7: Turn the repeated parts into Hermes skills, cron jobs, scripts, and delivery workflows.
