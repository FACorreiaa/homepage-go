### 1. 🔴 Bitcoin Crashes Below $66K as US-Iran Strikes Resume
Bitcoin dropped 6.4% to $65,385 on Coinbase, the largest single-day drop since February. The sell-off was triggered by fresh US-Iran strikes, but amplified by $1.83B in liquidations, $3.97B in cumulative ETF outflows, and technical breakdowns. Support at $65,000 is critical — a break below could send BTC to $60,000.

### 2. 🏛️ US Treasury Sanctions 4 Iranian Crypto Exchanges (Nobitex)
The Treasury's "Economic Fury" campaign added Nobitex, Wallex, Bitpin, and Ramzinex to OFAC's sanctions list. Chainalysis identified Nobitex as handling ~50% of Iran's crypto volume. Treasury Secretary Bessent confirmed nearly $1B in crypto seizures from Iran since February.
- **Sources:** CoinTelegraph (browser-extracted), The Block

### 3. 📋 SEC Makes Digital Assets Strategic Priority Through 2030
The SEC's draft Strategic Plan for FY 2026-2030 dedicates a full objective to digital assets — a first in agency history. It calls for regulatory clarity on tokenization, custody, staking, and onchain infrastructure. Plan also aims to resolve SEC/CFTC jurisdictional overlap, directly supporting the CLARITY Act.
- **Sources:** CoinTelegraph (browser-extracted), Bitcoin.com

### 4. 🏦 Bitcoin ETF Outflows Hit Record Streak ($3.97B)
Spot Bitcoin ETFs recorded 12 consecutive days of net outflows. BlackRock's IBIT alone has shed ~$440M+ per recent session. Standard Chartered warns sustained outflows could see BTC retest $50K. K33 Research notes CME Bitcoin futures OI dropped to its lowest since Oct 2023.
- **Sources:** The Block, FXStreet, CoinMarketCap

### 5. 🛡️ Blockchain Association Urges Senate to Pass Clarity Act
Letter signed by 160 former security officials urges Senate to pass the Digital Asset Market Clarity Act. The bill advanced from Senate Banking Committee last month and is expected on the Senate floor. Virtual town hall planned for Thursday.

## Key Themes

### Capital Rotation Out of Crypto
The dominant narrative is capital rotating from crypto into AI/semiconductor equities. Global stock indexes hit fresh records while crypto suffered its worst week since February. This is the most significant structural headwind for crypto since the start of the institutional bull run.

### Geopolitical Risk (US-Iran)
Fresh US-Iran strikes coincided with Bitcoin's breakdown below $70K. While analysts attribute most of the price action to liquidation cascades and ETF outflows, geopolitical uncertainty is acting as an accelerant.

### Regulatory Acceleration
The SEC's strategic plan, the Clarity Act advancement, and the SEC-CFTC MOU all point to a coordinated effort to establish clear crypto regulation before 2026 ends. This is net-positive for the industry long-term but may not help short-term price action.

### DeFi Risk Reassessment
Aave's post-exploit overhaul is a signal to the entire industry: DeFi risk management must extend beyond smart contract audits to include bridge infrastructure, operational security, and cross-chain dependencies.
