Global crypto investment products issued by asset managers such as BlackRock, Fidelity, and 21Shares shed $1.47 billion last week, a second consecutive negative week and the third-largest weekly outflow of 2026, according to CoinShares.

Cumulative redemptions over the two-week stretch now stand at $2.54 billion, with Iran-linked risk-off deepening and spreading across “virtually every region” despite continued progress on the Clarity Act, CoinShares Head of Research James Butterfill wrote in the firm's weekly [report](https://researchblog.coinshares.com/volume-287-digital-asset-fund-flows-weekly-report-e12b92aa9cbd) on Tuesday.

The prior week — which ended a six-week positive streak — [saw $1.07 billion](https://www.theblock.co/post/401649/weekly-crypto-etp-outflows-top-1b-ending-six-week-positive-streak-amid-iran-linked-risk-off-coinshares) in outflows. Total assets under management across tracked products stood at $148.7 billion, with last week's outflows representing roughly 1% of total AUM.
