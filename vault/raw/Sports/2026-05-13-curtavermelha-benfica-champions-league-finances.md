# Benfica's Financial Strain Without Champions League Revenue

**Author:** Coluna Vermelha (@CurtaVermelha)  
**Source:** X/Twitter (Portuguese)  
**Date:** 2026-05-13

## Summary


Portuguese deep-dive into Benfica's financial strain after missing the Champions League. Revenue loss of €30-50M, plus needing ~€60M net to service past debt. Highlights excessive FSE costs (€38M H1 vs Sporting €29M, Porto €25M), stadium operational costs (~€31.5M/season managed by opaque Benfica Estádio company), and bloated player wages (€49M H1 vs Porto €31M, Sporting €37M). Calls for cost-cutting, particularly in stadium management and player salaries. Notes potential conflict of interest: former Benfica exec Miguel Moreira allegedly negotiated the stadium maintenance contract and later bought the contractor company for €100M+ in 3.5 years.


## Key Figures Mentioned

- Champions League participation revenue: ~€53M (Benfica)
- Europa League participation revenue: ~€25M (Braga)
- FSE (H1): Benfica €38M vs Sporting €29M vs Porto €25M
- Stadium operational cost (season): ~€31.5M
- Stadium revenue (season): ~€47M
- Player wages (H1): Benfica €49M vs Porto €31M vs Sporting €37M
- Variable compensation (no UCL): €4M (2020/21) vs €10-11M (current)
- Total funding gap: €90-110M needed
